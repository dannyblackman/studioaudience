Four considered concepts. Each shown at scale with lockup, on light and dark, and at small sizes.
Concept A
studio audience
Close to your reference. Three data rows, one dot displaced and coloured — the outlier insight. The offset is subtle, just enough to break the grid.
Concept B
studio audience
2×2 grid. Circle and pill alternate diagonally. The bottom-right dot is orange — the finding. Feels like a dashboard widget. Confident, balanced, slightly playful.
Concept C
studio audience
Three bars growing in length. The middle insight bar is orange. Reads as a chart, a signal growing, or three lines of a research brief. Very minimal.
Concept D
studio audience
Two dots, one bridge. The pill connects them. Two data points become a finding. Diagonal energy makes it feel dynamic. The orange dot is the discovery.

These are directional. Once you pick one, the proportions, spacing, and colour balance should be refined in Figma where you can nudge things pixel by pixel. SVG gets us the concept — Figma gets us the finish.
